List of Substitution Ciphers

  • Caesar Cipher: Shifts the alphabet by a fixed number of positions.
  • Vigenère Cipher: Uses a keyword to shift letters differently throughout the message.
  • Monoalphabetic Cipher: Uses a fixed substitution over the entire message, where each letter of the plaintext is mapped to a corresponding letter of the ciphertext alphabet.
  • Polyalphabetic Cipher: Uses multiple substitution alphabets to encrypt the message, making it more secure than monoalphabetic ciphers.
  • Playfair Cipher: Uses a 5x5 grid of letters to encrypt digraphs (pairs of letters).
  • Hill Cipher: Uses linear algebra and matrix multiplication to encrypt messages.
